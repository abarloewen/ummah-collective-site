The firm

They dig before
Bavaria builds.

Dig it! Company GbR is a freelance field-archaeology team working across Bavaria since 2001 — excavating and documenting for public institutions and private developers before construction can begin, from four offices: Peiting (HQ), Garching, Fürstenfeldbruck and Landsberg. The name is the work: archaeologists who dig. Their 2018-era website said none of that.

The build

Precise like
a Feldplan.

We presented three complete design concepts as working demos; the firm chose Feldplan — a light survey-blueprint system with paper grid, slab type and drawn section profiles. Because the original photography was low-resolution, the design is deliberately image-light: SVG survey motifs carry the identity instead. Underneath: three languages including full Arabic RTL, and the complete 70-project archive from seventeen years of digs.

What we built

System · languages · archive

The Feldplan system

A survey-blueprint design language — paper grid, slab type, datum points and section drawings. Bold and angular, no clichés.

Three concepts to choose from

Strata, Feldplan and Monolith — three full design directions built as working demos, not moodboards. The client chose Feldplan.

Trilingual, full RTL

German, English and Arabic with complete right-to-left support — rare for a regional Handwerk-adjacent firm, deliberate for international clients.

70-project archive

The complete reference list 2001–2017, structured as data and rendered in all three languages — seventeen years of work, browsable.

Image-light by design

The original photos were low-resolution — so the system replaces them with drawn SVG survey motifs that will never age or pixelate.

Shipped & live

Six pages, Google-Maps contact, social integration and the real company emblem — deployed on Vercel.

Three concepts, one call

Design as a decision, not a guess

Instead of one take-it-or-leave-it draft, we built three full design directions as clickable demos — dark-cinematic, survey-blueprint, and brutalist — so the decision was made on real pages, not promises. Feldplan won because it looks like what the firm actually does: precise documentation of the ground beneath Bavaria.

  • Konzept AStrata — dark, photographic, cinematic
  • Konzept BFeldplan — light survey-blueprint, bold & angular
  • Konzept CMonolith — brutalist, type-led
  • EntscheidungFeldplan — precise like the work itself

Seventeen years, structured
